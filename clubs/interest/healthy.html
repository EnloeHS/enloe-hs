Healthy Initiatives Club

What is the Healthy Initiatives Club?

This club aims to focus on psychological, health, and educational benefits that are a natural result of making physical activity and healthy food habits part of our daily lives. Throughout the year, we will learn not only about health, but also about its connections to public health, anatomy and physiology, the climate, and countless other branches of science. We will work to make Enloe a healthier school by focusing on aspects of health including safety policies, physical education, and health promotion for staff. Through this work, we hope to receive national recognition from Alliance for a Healthier Generation, and be the first high school to apply for the Advocates for Health in Action's Brains and Bodies Award. We also hope to spread awareness of debilitating diseases and disorders that can be prevented with a simple lifestyle change and educate members of our club and our club on the human body, public health, a healthy lifestyle and how they can spread the word. Education and incentive can save lives.

Who can join the Healthy Initiatives Club?

The Healthy Initiatives Club is for anyone and everyone. Whether you are interested in the science side of health, want to lead a healthier lifestyle, or just want to see what this club is about, go ahead and come out to our meetings.

What will I get out of this club?

  • This club will give you valuable information on leading a long, healthy life. It may not seem important now, but a few years down the road, the decisions you make today regarding your health will define your life.
  • You will get a chance to learn about multiple branches of science that relate to health, such as public health, anatomy and physiology, and even climate.
  • I will work with you to ensure that you receive service hours for the time you dedicate to make Enloe a healthier school during club meetings.
  • You will receive valuable information about opportunities outside of school regarding health and wellness.

What will meetings be like?

Meetings will generally be held once a month in Ms. Welty’s room, 609.They will last about an hour after school, from 2:45 pm to 3:45 pm. All meeting dates will be announced on the 'LoeDown. We will have a variety of guest speakers representing organizations concerning different aspects of health, and will plan and execute projects around the school to contribute to our application to the American Heart Association’s Brains and Bodies Award, as well as national recognition by the Alliance for a Healthier Generation . As of now, meetings will most probably be held on Mondays or Thursdays, to better accommodate our speakers.
